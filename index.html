Chameleon

  1. Chameleons are reptiles that are part of the iguana suborder.
  2. Changing skin colour is an important part of communication amoung chameleons.
  3. Most chameleons have a prehensile tail that they use to wrap around the tree branches.

Camel

  • Camel's ears are furry.
  • Camel can move easily across the sand because of its specially designed feet.
  • When they find water, they will drink as much as possible.

Pigeon

  1. Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals.
  2. Pigeons are renowned for their outstanding navigational abilities.
  3. Pigeons have excellent hearing abilities.

Teddy Bear

  • Teddy bears got their name from the story that Teddy Roosevelt refused to shoot a bear cub while on a hunting trip in 1902.
  • The first toy stuffed bear was created by German toymaker Margarete Steiff. She created a soft plush with movable arms and legs and a friendly face after taking her nephew to the zoo in 1903.

Panda

  • Giant pandas are good at climbing trees and can also swim.
  • Pandas go from pink to white and black (or brown).
  • Pandas have so many fans because they look cute.

Frog

  • Frogs don't need to drink water as they absorb it through their skin.
  • A frog's call is unique to its species, and some frog calls can be heard up to a mile away.
  • Some frogs can jump over 20 times their own body length; that is like a human jumping 30m.
